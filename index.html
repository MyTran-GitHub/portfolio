The vision
"How do we see the invisible?"

A satellite sees 13 spectral bands where the eye sees three. The work is teaching a model to read semantic environmental structure out of that invisible spectrum.

PyTorch TensorFlow Sentinel-2 U-Net ViT

How do we teach a computer to see what an ecologist sees? We moved beyond pixel-wise classification because standard CNNs lost spatial continuity across cloud shadows — forcing a pivot toward multi-spectral U-Net segmentation that preserves shape and edge, then validating against hand-labelled masks.

The map
"Where does intervention matter most?"

While the 'Vision' tells us what is happening, the 'Map' tells us where to act. I build spatial engines that transforms coarse climate data into pixel-level suitability maps that pinpoint exactly where biophysical signatures align for the highest spatial return on investment. Drag the dials to model where shifting to AWD irrigation can reduce water use by 60% and methane emissions by 24%.

Google Earth Engine Rasterio Xarray GeoPandas

A water-saving policy that works in the Mekong isn't automatically portable. We built a water-balance suitability model over hydrological index and topographic slope — because fragmented, sloped terrain quietly breaks the assumptions that made AWD succeed at home.

The engineer
"Standardizing the chaos of Earth’s history."

I bridge the gap between raw satellite imagery and decision-ready insights by architecting cloud-native geospatial pipelines. My work spans from implementing STAC-based discovery for cloud-optimized storage (AWS S3), leveraging parallel Xarray/Dask for scalable compute, to utilizing GeoParquet for columnar serialization of satellite embeddings. I don't just process data; I engineer the infrastructure that makes 25 years of planetary change accessible, reproducible, and ready for model-scale inference.

AWS S3 STAC Dask Xarray HPC (TACC)

Why out-of-core Dask chunking over standard in-memory arrays? Because a 25-year Landsat archive silently exceeds RAM and the naïve pipeline dies at the memory boundary. Lazy, chunked graphs let the same laptop reason over continental extents.

The truth
"What is the causal impact of our interventions?"

I bridge the frontline research between GeoAI and Causal Inference to solve the problem of unseen counterfactual. I use Foundation Model embeddings to identify ecologically identical 'digital twins,' allowing me to isolate the true causal effect of environmental interventions from the background noise of climate variability. I don't just ask 'What changed?', I ask 'Why did it change, and was our intervention the reason?'

AlphaEarth CausalImagesR Landsat PyTorch

Satellite embeddings (AlphaEarth) let us pick donor regions that are physically similar, not just statistically convenient. Embedding-matched controls cut pre-treatment error by 49% — so the residual gap reflects the policy, not the noise.

The loop closes when the science reaches the people who act on it. Fuego.Earth delivers sub-minute wildfire simulations to community responders. Click a cell to ignite — watch the wind carry it.

Cell2Fire LANDFIRE FastAPI React · Mapbox
